# AI Governance Frameworks Under Pressure

Analysis of the frameworks that claim to govern frontier AI, read by a crisis and emergency management practitioner through three lenses: governance, resilience, and decision-making. One operational question is held front and centre throughout: **does the framework hold when a real decision has to be made fast, under pressure?**

This repository is the published layer of a larger private research corpus that tracks eighteen frameworks across eight clusters, from binding regulation (EU AI Act, the Council of Europe Framework Convention) through standards (the ISO/IEC 42001 family) and national approaches (NIST, UK, UAE) to the frontier labs' self-governance frameworks. What appears here is the finished analytical work: framework one-pagers and comparative artefacts, frozen at close and dated. The working record behind them (a living cross-framework synthesis, reasoning notes, and the corpus master index) is held privately. A public key to the numbered threads, instruments and verification apparatus those artefacts cite is in [METHOD.md](METHOD.md).

## The flagship

**[Delay, Halt, Nothing: The Frontier-Lab Safety Frameworks Under Pressure](comparative-artefacts/Comparative_Frontier_Labs_10July2026.md)** (10 July 2026)

A three-lens comparative of the Anthropic Responsible Scaling Policy (v3.3), the OpenAI Preparedness Framework (v2), and the Google DeepMind Frontier Safety Framework (v3.1). The title is the finding, established by word-boundary search of the three primary texts: asked what happens when a capability line is crossed, one framework says delay, one says halt, and one contains no stopping word at all.

The comparative runs all three frameworks down the same four-stage pipeline (define the capability line, detect the crossing, decide the response, be made to act) and finds the three labs hollow at three different stages, so no two fail in the same place and an overlay of all three covers the whole pipeline with hollows. Four absences hold across the entire cluster: no external on-switch, no response clock on any model, no structurally different machinery for the hazards that corrupt the labs' own evidence base, and nothing anywhere that holds both outcome substance and enforcement machinery.

## Method

The corpus is built by sequential deep-dives, one framework at a time, each closing with a frozen one-pager and a private reasoning note. Cross-framework findings accumulate in a living synthesis file and surface publicly only once verified; the flagship comparative was gated behind a full consistency pass across the corpus before a line of it was drafted.

Three reusable instruments developed by the deep-dives carry the comparative work:

- **The failability test.** For every obligation, ask whether the actor could demonstrably fail it. Unfailable obligations are aspirations wearing commitment clothing. Applied across the frontier cluster the test needed three bins, not two: failable commitments, unfailable aspirations, and an underdetermined middle (real safeguards, genuinely run, that the text never compels), which in one framework holds the document's entire substantive centre.
- **The four-stage pipeline.** Every threshold-trigger framework is the same machine: define, detect, decide, enforce. Running frameworks down the stages locates where each goes soft, and often refuses a ranking in favour of a structural contrast.
- **The exits.** Every frontier framework carries pre-authorised routes around its own obligations. The instrument asks not how well-guarded each valve is, but which direction it opens and whether what it releases can be recalled.

Verification discipline: version currency is checked against primary sources at every dive open (content-verified, not version-string-verified); load-bearing textual claims are established by word-boundary search of the primary documents; artefacts are frozen at close, dated, superseded by stacking, and reopened only for factual error.

**Working method declaration.** The corpus is built iteratively with Claude (Anthropic): structural orientation, Socratic challenge, drafting, and source verification. The analytical judgements, every verdict-level formulation, and the final text are the analyst's. The assistance is declared as method, not caveat.

## What is not here

The framework texts themselves are not hosted in this repository. Redistribution rights vary, and a link to the source of record is better currency practice than a hosted copy that goes stale: see [SOURCES.md](SOURCES.md) for verified links to every primary document the artefacts analyse or cite. The private corpus records (master index, cross-framework synthesis, reasoning notes) are the working apparatus behind the published artefacts and are not published with them.
